Large donation from Canada

A large collection of Canadian files arrived to AFU a couple of days ago. Two pallets with UFO reports, books, magazines, newsletters, gadgets, recorded tapes collected over 30 years by several local ufologists saved by researchers Drew Williamson from the files of Tom Theofanous, Larry Fenwick (CUFORN), Bonnie Wheeler (Cambridge UFO Research Group), Hugh F. Cochrane, Larry Blazey (US researcher) and some material from different Ohio groups.
Fenwick was the editor of CUFORN bulletin. In total the donation was 650 kilos that arrived in Norrköping on a lorry after traveling over the Atlantic by boat.

Large donation from Canada on its way

Finally, the two pallets of UFO material are on their way from Toronto! I was almost starting to wonder if they would never be picked up but after a third change of local transport company came the message from the company AFU and I cooperate with in the US at 17.26 today (December 1st) – the pallets have started the journey to the port.

This process began on December 12, 2020 with the researcher Drew Williamson contacting me and wondering if AFU would be interested in a larger collection of UFO reports, books, magazines, newsletters, gadgets, recorded tapes collected over 30 years by several local ufologists: Tom Theofanous, Larry Fenwick (CUFORN), Bonnie Wheeler (Cambridge UFO Research Group), Hugh F. Cochrane, Larry Blazey (US researcher), some material from different Ohio groups and possibly some from Errol Bruce Knapp. Of course we were interested!

When I look back at the correspondence that Drew and I had during this time and the one that I had with the company that helps us with the transport, almost 200 emails have been exchanged. First so that Drew would feel safe with AFU taking care of everything in a good way and then with ordering pallets, plastic, transferring money and all the paperwork that goes with a transport across the Atlantic.

In the end, it got really problematic and it would take a week before the two pallets were picked up by a truck from Drew’s driveway in Toronto. Then it had snowed once but a tarpaulin and the plastic around the boxes protected it all. In total 650 kilos of material is now being sent to Sweden.

Nothing is easy when it comes to these transports but I am happy and proud that 650 kilos of UFO material will soon be on its way across the Atlantic. A big thank you to Rowena at the transport company in Florida and to Drew who saved the material and together with Grant Smales carried everything out and packed it.

Everyone contacting AFU for preservation of Fortean material can feel sure that we will take care of it in the best way. We are also paying for transport and all costs that comes with the packing.
