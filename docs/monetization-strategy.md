# UFOlogist Monetization Strategy

## Principle

UFOlogist should monetize research utility, not curiosity. The public atlas, core map, knowledge center, curated cases, and source links should remain open. Paid surfaces should help serious users work faster, organize evidence, and fund maintenance without making the app feel commercial.

## Product Model

The first realistic offer is `UFOlogist Research Pass`, positioned as early access for advanced research tools:

- Clean exports: extended CSV/JSON, per-case source bundles, prepared bibliographies.
- Advanced notebooks: collections, cross-notes, source tracking, and eventual sync.
- Alerts and dossiers: source updates, wave comparisons, curated downloadable dossiers.

Suggested founding price:

- Monthly: 4 EUR/month.
- Annual: 29 EUR/year.
- Founder: higher-support option for early supporters, manually handled at first.

## Launch Path

1. Validate demand with the lightweight in-app modal and email-based waitlist.
2. Track intent locally and by incoming email before adding payments.
3. If demand is real, connect Stripe Payment Links or Lemon Squeezy.
4. Move entitlements behind a simple hosted account system only after paid demand exists.

## Non-Invasive Rules

- No paywall on public data exploration.
- No blocking interstitial on launch.
- No ads in the core globe experience.
- No dark patterns around exports or sources.
- Always explain that paid features fund the project and improve research workflows.

## Future Implementation Notes

- Replace the current `mailto:` CTA with a hosted checkout or waitlist endpoint.
- Keep the pass button visually secondary in the top bar and mobile More menu.
- Add feature flags for pass-only exports before adding authentication.
- Use privacy-first analytics for clicks, plan choice, and completed checkout only.
- Consider sponsorships only for research institutions, books, podcasts, or tools aligned with the UFOlogist editorial tone.
